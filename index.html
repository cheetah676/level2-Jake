Lorax Clicker

by: Jake Mordan

This game was made for my level 2 project at The League of Amazing Programmers.
Click here to download and play!

1. Why I Made This Game

I decided to make this game because last summer I was at a surfing camp and my cabin was talking about the lorax and how he would probably come for us at night. It was pretty funny at the time, so I made this game. It's also really stupid, which is a plus.

2. What I Learned

I learned that nobody would really want to play my game for more than five minutes. I also was told to add percent of what the tax guy taxes rather than a set number.

3. Architecture of My Game

I have four individual tree images in my game, and when space is pressed and released, one appears and one disappears. The other two come when you upgrade to double trees. The only objects that move in my game are the trees and the tax guy, so the game is pretty still. The game is told by the timer and score, and not really by animation. My game panel has almost all of the code, and the other classes are just there for a little organization.

4. Technical Implementation

My game is basically cookie clicker with taxes. You click, you upgrade, and you lose all your points with tax. I'm proud of how I was able to find just the right amount of tax for the start and the finish as it increases over time. When I first started, I just put images and text on the screen, and as I progressed, I made things happen.

5. Most Challenging Part

The most challenging part of my project was when I needed to coordinate the timers and reset them precisely when the game starts and ends. I overcame this challenge by making two separate timers into one organized timer.

6. Future Fixes and Updates

If I were to keep working on the game, I would make more upgrades you can buy in the game. I would also add more sound affects to different aspects of the game to make it more exciting.
